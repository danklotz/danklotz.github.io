goblin footnotes

Nasty patterns I found while dabbling in the research community.

  • Experimental hollowness: When it becomes unclear if a benchmarking study, an empirical experiment or a simulation study contributes anything other than an improvement with regard to a under-specified metric (any perhaps even introduces with regard to some other relevant metrics).
  • Empirical death end: When a theory does not admit any (more) experiments that can lead to its refutation or advancement. Experimental hollowness (see above) is one indicator of an empirical death end. It can, however, also be a result from the theory as such, see for example the arguments in Sabine_Hossenfelder’s Lost in Math.
  • Winner’s forgetfulness: Winning induces forgetfulness. Winners always forget faster than losers. They also often tend to spin events post-hoc in ways that make things look either rational, unavoidable or a different light – and then believe in it!
  • Beveridge (1957): The art of scientific investigation
  • Hamming (1986): You and Your Research
