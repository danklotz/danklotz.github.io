rr: distributional predictions

Notes on distributional predictions for rainfall-runoff (r-r) modelling.

Point predictions are often of limited value. This is especially the case if only view predictions are made (maybe just one). Generally, the problem is that points equate to a prediction with very low uncertainty and/or an error that is extremely well behaved. Both are seldom the case. The solution is either (a) to make more coarse predictions — e.g. the runoff will be higher or lower than in the previous day — or (b) to make richer predictions that can better account for the uncertainty — e.g. providing bounds or even full distributions.

In r-r modelling strategy (b) is often pursued by adapting point-predictions. First a (deterministic) model is used to make a point predictions; then a second approach is layered on top of it to get an uncertainty estimates. This latter scheme might be a pre-processor, an updating-scheme or a post-processor etc.
